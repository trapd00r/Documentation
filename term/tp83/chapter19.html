Chapter 19

Cables

Introduction

This chapter describes the most popular cables required for connecting your video terminal, printing terminal and printer to processor buses, communication options and other peripherals in both local and remote applications.

The first section contains a series of interconnect cable tables, 19-1 through 19-6, for current and traditional products. These tables will help you find the cable needed for your application.

The second section, Cable Descriptions, discusses the key features of the more popular cables and includes ordering information. EIA, 20 mA and general purpose cables are featured.

The third section briefly describes some traditional cable products.

The final section describes some of the more popular connector kits.

While this chapter lists many of Digital's most popular cables, it is not intended to be comprehensive. More information can be found in the Cables Handbook For The System Builder. Contact your sales representative or Digital's Installed Base Group for more information.

Cable Descriptions

The following cables are commonly used for interconnection purposes. More details can be found in the Cables Handbook for the System Builder.

BCC04 Modem Cable

The BCC04 is the modem cable specifically designed for use with the VT200 family of video terminals and the personal computer product family. The cable is used to connect any personal computer with a modem and for Rainbow 100 only to a printer.

Its features include:

  • Fully shielded cable and connector assembly
  • Molded-on strain relief
  • Connectors: one male and one female, 25-position, with D-subminiature molded-on construction

The following are the part numbers and lengths available for the BCC04 cable assembly.

BCC05 Printer Cable

The BCC05 is the printer cable designed specifically for Digital's VT200 video terminal and personal computer families. The cable is used to connect any personal computer with any of the specified Digital printers.

Its features include:

  • Connectors: one 9-position female D-subminiature and one 25-position female D-subminiature

BC03M Null Modem Cable

The BC03M is a null modem cable for local EIA connections. If features a 25-pin RS232 female connector at each end. The cable can be used with Digital processors as well as many processors not manufactured by Digital Equipment Corporation. A typical hook-up is one end to the male connector of the EIA processor interface cable and the other end to a male connector of an EIA terminal.

Its features include:

  • Individually shielded conductors
  • 3-TWP to reduce cross-talk on signal lines
  • Compact, rugged construction
  • Connectors: two RS232 female connectors
  • Ideally suited for long-length applications

BC05C Input/Output Cable

The BC05C is an EIA input/output cable used for asynchronous, serial line unit interfacing. The cable can be used for full or limited modem control applications. The H856 connector mates with the computer interface, and the RS232 male mates with a modem device or null modem connection.

Its features include:

  • Built-in strain relief
  • Compact, rugged
  • Connectors: H856 and RS232 (male)

BC05M Cable Assembly

The BC05M is a current loop input/output cable assembly. It is used with Digital processors for 20 mA current loop interfacing applications. Its three-twisted pairs make it ideal for applications where minimizing cross-talk is necessary. Its performance approaches that of a coaxial cable, but it costs less, is more compact, and is more flexible.

Its features include:

  • Shielded
  • Built-in strain relief
  • Connectors: H856 connector at one end and a MATE-N-LOK connector at the other; both are female connectors

BC22A Null Modem Cable

This cable is a null modem cable assembly especially designed for connections to asynchronous EIA terminals such as the VT100, LA120 and the LA34. It connects these terminals directly to the male connector on the distribution panel of a communications option or to the cable from a serial processor interface.

Its features include:

  • Tough, molded-on connectors for long life, reliability, compactness and easy grip
  • Braided shield to minimize cross-talk
  • Connectors: one EIA RS232 female connector at each end

BC22D Null Modem Cable

The BC22D is a fully shielded, null modem cable. It is designed to be used in asynchronous applications only. Its shielding characteristics have been tested and proven to limit RFI/EMI emissions in excess of industry standards. The cable has female RS232 molded-on connectors on each end.

Its features include:

  • Braided and foil shields
  • Shielding effectiveness: 30 dB minimum in 30 MHz-200 MHz range
  • Rugged molded-on connectors
  • Backward compatible with BC03M and BC22A
  • Designed to be used with the DMF32 and DESCA
  • Wide variety of standard lengths for flexible system configuration
  • Connectors: two female, D-subminiature

Connector Kits
